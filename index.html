EvDehaze: Event-Guided Image Dehazing under Adverse Weather Conditions


  • Dataset

Abstract

EvDehaze is a novel event-guided image restoration framework designed to address challenging dehazing problems under dynamic, adverse weather conditions such as rain, fog, and low-light. Leveraging the high temporal resolution and motion sensitivity of event cameras, EvDehaze introduces a dual-branch architecture that injects event-aware priors via cross-modal attention mechanisms to refine visibility and scene fidelity. We demonstrate superior performance of EvDehaze across various weather types on real-world and synthetic benchmarks.

System Overview

Architecture Details

The network consists of an event encoder that processes temporal event stacks into feature maps, which are then fused with RGB image features in a UNet backbone via cross-attention layers. This facilitates effective alignment of structural and motion cues from events with color textures in hazy scenes.

Applications

EvDehaze is applicable to real-time perception in autonomous driving, robotics, and surveillance, especially in scenarios with compromised visibility. The fusion of event-based sensors with conventional RGB imaging opens a new direction in robust vision systems.
